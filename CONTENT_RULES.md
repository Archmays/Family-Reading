# Content Rules

## Companion Structure

Each book gets one companion entrance. The companion helps family reading beside the paper book; it does not replace the book.

For picture books, use `scene` or `pageRange` to organize content. Do not force a chapter model when the book does not have chapters.

## Required Book Companion Content

Each book companion should include:

- Book overview
- Story review
- Question cards
- Background notes
- Story-related encyclopedia entries
- Audio playback

## Question Types

Question cards should be grouped into:

- Factual recall: what happened, who appeared, where an event happened.
- Comprehension: why something happened, how a character felt, what changed.
- Open expression: child-friendly speaking, drawing, imagining, or personal response prompts.

The tone should support parent-child conversation, not test-taking.

## Encyclopedia Rules

Encyclopedia entries must connect directly to the story, characters, places, objects, actions, or knowledge points in the book.

Avoid generic encyclopedia expansion that is not anchored in the plot.

## OCR Rules

OCR text is a processing aid. Do not put OCR full text directly into the child-facing main interface.

Use OCR results to understand scenes, characters, story beats, vocabulary, and background knowledge. The final companion text should be summarized, reviewed, and family-friendly.
